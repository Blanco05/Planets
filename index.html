Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, distinguished by its abundant liquid water and life-supporting atmosphere. Composed of a solid crust, mantle, and core, it has a unique magnetic field and supports diverse ecosystems due to its moderate climate and orbit. Earth's atmosphere, rich in nitrogen and oxygen, also shields it from solar radiation, sustaining life.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, known for its red appearance due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It has a thin atmosphere, primarily carbon dioxide, and features the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, and scientists believe it may have hosted liquid water in its ancient past.


Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has the shortest orbital period, completing one orbit in just 88 days. It has a rocky, heavily cratered surface with extreme temperature fluctuations due to its thin atmosphere. Mercury's lack of an atmosphere and its proximity to the Sun make it one of the hottest and most inhospitable planets in the solar system.


Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and has a thick, toxic atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. It has extreme surface temperatures, averaging around 465Â°C, hotter than Mercury despite being farther from the Sun. Venus's surface is mostly volcanic plains, and its slow rotation makes a day longer than a year on the planet.


Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its massive size and strong magnetic field. It is a gas giant with a composition mainly of hydrogen and helium, and it features a famous storm called the Great Red Spot. Jupiter has at least 79 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system.


Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is famous for its prominent ring system made of ice and rock particles. It is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with a low density that allows it to float in water. Saturn has over 80 moons, with Titan being the largest.


Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has a unique tilt, rotating on its side at an angle of about 98 degrees. Uranus has 27 known moons and a faint ring system.


Neptun

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and is a gas giant known for its striking blue color, caused by methane in its atmosphere. It has strong winds, the fastest in the solar system, and 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest.
